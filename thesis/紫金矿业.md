## Executive Summary

Zijin Mining is the portfolio's primary listed resource-equity position.

The thesis is based on diversified exposure to gold and copper, relatively durable resource quality, and the portfolio need for a real-asset allocation that is separate from consumer-internet and technology concentration.

This is an equity thesis, not a substitute for gold accumulation.

## Why We Own It

We own Zijin Mining because we believe:

- it provides diversified exposure to gold and copper rather than a single-metal bet
- resource exposure improves overall portfolio balance against growth concentration
- gold remains supported by long-term macro conditions including debt pressure, reserve diversification, and geopolitical uncertainty
- copper has structural demand support from AI infrastructure, electrification, power grid upgrades, and industrial automation
- Zijin offers a more operationally scalable way to express the resource theme than holding only a passive commodity view

## Investment Drivers

### 1. Gold Plus Copper Exposure

Zijin should not be treated as a pure gold proxy.

Its value comes from combined exposure to precious metals and industrial metals, which creates a broader resource thesis than a single-commodity trade.

### 2. Portfolio Diversification

Rachel Capital is still concentrated in growth-oriented equity exposure.

Zijin adds a different earnings and macro sensitivity profile, which can improve portfolio resilience when internet-platform or consumer-growth names face pressure.

### 3. Structural Copper Demand

Copper demand has a multi-year support case through AI data centers, transmission infrastructure, EV supply chains, and robotics.

If this demand remains durable, Zijin benefits from more than defensive gold logic alone.

### 4. Real-Asset Optionality

In a world of policy uncertainty, debt expansion, and periodic inflation scares, resource equities can preserve strategic optionality that purely domestic growth portfolios often lack.

## Risks

Major risks include:

- short-term gold correction
- weaker-than-expected copper demand
- commodity-price volatility overwhelming company execution
- policy or geopolitical disruptions affecting overseas assets
- cyclical drawdowns causing equity beta to exceed the expected defensive role

## Separation From Gold Accumulation

Gold accumulation and Zijin Mining must remain separate decisions.

| Asset | Type | Decision Logic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gold (`积存金` / Gold ETF) | Asset allocation tool | Used for long-term reserve behavior, portfolio stability, and macro hedging. |
| Zijin Mining | Single-stock equity thesis | Depends on company execution, capital allocation, metal mix, and equity-market pricing. |

A better gold outlook does not automatically justify a larger Zijin position, and a better Zijin setup does not replace the need for direct gold allocation.

## Add Conditions

Increase position when:

- the gold and copper thesis remains intact
- valuation or price pullback improves the entry point
- portfolio resource exposure is still below the desired long-term range
- the add improves diversification without crowding out higher-conviction core holdings

Probe adds are preferred over aggressive one-step sizing.

## Reduce Conditions

Reduce position if:

- the gold or copper thesis weakens materially
- the stock becomes too large relative to the quality of conviction
- resource exposure can be replaced more effectively by direct gold accumulation or better opportunities
- company execution or capital allocation deteriorates

Short-term volatility alone is not a sufficient reason to exit.
