# Berkshire

This file tracks Berkshire Hathaway as an asset-allocation and decision-discipline reference, not as a direct stock-copying model.

## Why Track It

Berkshire is useful for observing:

- cash ratio discipline
- concentrated sizing
- trimming behavior
- preference for financials, energy, and consumer assets
- what patient capital does when opportunity quality is scarce

## What Matters Most

- cash levels
- top position concentration
- meaningful reductions
- sector preference changes
- capital deployment pace

## Interpretation Rules

- Berkshire is most useful as a capital-allocation teacher, not as a stock list.
- Cash restraint can be as informative as a new purchase.
- A trim by Berkshire does not automatically mean sell; it may reflect valuation, taxes, or portfolio concentration.
- Rachel Capital should translate Berkshire observations into portfolio principles, not mimic tickers mechanically.
