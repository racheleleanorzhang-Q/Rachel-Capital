# Institution Tracker

Track long-term institutional capital flows, including Social Security Fund, Central Huijin, Berkshire Hathaway, public funds, insurers, foreign funds, and other long-term investors.

This module is used as supporting evidence for watchlist updates, portfolio allocation, and decision logs. It is not a signal-following or copy-trading system.

## Purpose

The institution tracker exists to answer five questions:

1. Which sectors are long-term capital allocators increasing or defending?
2. Does the Rachel Capital watchlist align with durable institutional direction?
3. Which names are being accumulated quietly before broad market attention?
4. Does an institution move strengthen or weaken a future decision case?
5. Are we reacting to price, or to better evidence?

## How Rachel Capital Uses This Module

- `watchlist/Watchlist.md`: institutional activity can strengthen or weaken continued coverage.
- `strategy/Asset_Allocation.md`: institutional direction can support asset-class sizing judgment.
- `decisions/Decision_Log.md`: institutional signals can serve as one input to buy, add, reduce, or defer decisions.

Institutional activity is evidence, not authority.

## Mechanisms Absorbed From `ai-berkshire`

This module intentionally absorbs a few process ideas from `xbtlin/ai-berkshire`, but keeps Rachel Capital smaller and more personal in scope.

### 1. Forced Conclusion

Every important institution observation should end with a conclusion:

- strengthens the case
- weakens the case
- mixed / insufficient

No record should end as a purely balanced summary with no practical judgment.

### 2. Four Internal Lenses

Each important record should be tested from four angles:

- `Business`: what does the institution move imply about business quality or industry structure?
- `Valuation`: does the move suggest value, crowding, or an overheated trade?
- `Risk`: what is the strongest reason the institution could be wrong?
- `Portfolio`: does this improve Rachel Capital portfolio construction or merely add noise?

### 3. Anti-Bias Discipline

Each meaningful record should include:

- the strongest counterargument
- what evidence would falsify the interpretation
- an explicit uncertainty statement when information is incomplete
- an information quality rating: `A / B / C`

### 4. Data Rigor

- Important numbers should carry a source note.
- Important data points should be cross-checked against at least two sources when practical.
- Units, currency, position size basis, and date basis should be explicit.
- Vague wording should not replace numbers when numbers are available.

## Operating Workflow

1. Capture the institution action.
2. Record the affected stocks, sectors, and source.
3. Force a conclusion instead of stopping at description.
4. Decide whether `Watchlist`, `Asset_Allocation`, or `Decision_Log` needs follow-up.
5. Set a follow-up trigger or review date.
